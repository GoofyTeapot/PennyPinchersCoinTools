Wheat Pennies (1909–1958)

The Wheat cent — also called the Wheatback or Lincoln Wheat penny — is one of the most collected U.S. coins. Minted from 1909 to 1958, these coins are 95% copper and feature the iconic wheat stalks on the reverse.

Quick Facts

Years minted1909–1958
Metal95% copper
Weight3.11 grams
DesignerVictor D. Brenner

Mintmarks

Wheat pennies were minted at three locations:

  • No mintmark — Philadelphia
  • D — Denver
  • S — San Francisco

How to Identify a Wheat Penny

  • Reverse shows two wheat stalks
  • Obverse shows Lincoln’s portrait
  • Year is on the front
  • Mintmark is under the date
  • Weight should be around 3.11 g
