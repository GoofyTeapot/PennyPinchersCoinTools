Rare U.S. Coins

Some U.S. coins are rare because of low mintages, major errors, or unusual historical events. These coins are highly sought after by collectors and can be worth hundreds, thousands, or even millions of dollars.

Why Coins Become Rare

  • Low mintage — few were made
  • Mint errors — doubled dies, off‑centers, wrong planchets
  • Historical events — metal shortages, wartime changes
  • High demand — collectors drive up value
  • Destruction — many coins melted or lost

How to Spot a Rare Coin

  • Check the year and mintmark
  • Look for errors (doubling, off‑center, missing mintmark)
  • Weigh the coin — wrong planchets are valuable
  • Inspect the condition — higher grades = higher value
  • Compare with known key dates
